Module 2: Self-Knowledge

"Know thyself" - the ancient Greek maxim that remains the foundation of all wisdom. In this module, we explore the depths of self-understanding and develop practical tools for self-discovery.

Lesson 2.1: Understanding Your Mind

The Architecture of Mind

Your mind is a complex system with multiple layers and functions:

Conscious Mind

  • Current awareness and attention
  • Active thinking and reasoning
  • Immediate decision-making
  • Working memory

Unconscious Processes

  • Automatic behaviors and habits
  • Implicit memories and associations
  • Emotional reactions and intuitions
  • Pattern recognition

Cognitive Biases and Blind Spots

We all have systematic errors in thinking that affect our self-knowledge:

  1. Confirmation Bias: Seeking information that confirms our existing beliefs
  2. Dunning-Kruger Effect: Overestimating our competence in areas where we lack expertise
  3. Fundamental Attribution Error: Attributing our failures to circumstances but others' to character
  4. Optimism Bias: Believing we're less likely to experience negative events

Self-Observation Exercise

For the next 24 hours, notice your thoughts without judgment. What patterns do you observe?

Lesson 2.2: Emotional Intelligence

The Four Domains of Emotional Intelligence

1. Self-Awareness

Recognizing and understanding your own emotions as they occur.

  • Emotional awareness
  • Accurate self-assessment
  • Self-confidence

2. Self-Management

Managing your emotions effectively and adapting to change.

  • Emotional self-control
  • Adaptability
  • Achievement orientation
  • Positive outlook

3. Social Awareness

Understanding others' emotions and the dynamics in your organization.

  • Empathy
  • Organizational awareness
  • Service orientation

4. Relationship Management

Influencing, coaching, and mentoring others, and resolving conflict.

  • Influence
  • Coach and mentor
  • Conflict management
  • Team leadership

The Emotion Wheel

Understanding the nuances of emotions helps develop emotional granularity:

  • Joy: Happy, elated, content, satisfied, pleased
  • Sadness: Disappointed, discouraged, dejected, melancholy
  • Anger: Frustrated, irritated, annoyed, furious
  • Fear: Anxious, worried, nervous, terrified
  • Surprise: Amazed, astonished, startled, shocked
  • Disgust: Repulsed, revolted, contemptuous

Lesson 2.3: Values and Purpose

Discovering Your Core Values

Values are the fundamental beliefs that guide your decisions and behavior. They represent what is most important to you.

Common Core Values

• Authenticity
• Adventure
• Balance
• Compassion
• Creativity
• Excellence
• Family
• Freedom
• Growth
• Health
• Integrity
• Knowledge
• Leadership
• Love
• Peace
• Security
• Service
• Wisdom

Finding Your Purpose

Your purpose is the intersection of:

  1. What you love (Passion)
  2. What you're good at (Talent)
  3. What the world needs (Service)
  4. What you can be paid for (Profession)

Values Clarification Exercise

List your top 5 values and explain why each is important to you:

Living Aligned with Your Values

Signs of value alignment:

  • Feeling energized by your activities
  • A sense of authenticity and integrity
  • Reduced internal conflict
  • Greater life satisfaction
  • Clear decision-making

When values conflict:

  • Acknowledge the tension
  • Prioritize based on context
  • Look for creative solutions
  • Accept trade-offs when necessary

Module Exercises

Exercise 1: The Five Whys

Choose a recent decision you made. Ask "why" five times to uncover deeper motivations:

  1. What decision did you make?
  2. Why did you make that choice?
  3. Why was that important?
  4. Why does that matter to you?
  5. Why is that fundamental to who you are?
  6. What core value or belief did you discover?

Exercise 2: Emotional Check-In

Set three daily alarms. When they go off, record:

  • What emotion am I feeling?
  • What triggered this emotion?
  • How is it affecting my behavior?
  • What do I need right now?

Exercise 3: Personal Mission Statement

Write a one-paragraph statement that captures your purpose and values. Include:

  • Who you are
  • What you stand for
  • What you want to contribute
  • How you want to be remembered
